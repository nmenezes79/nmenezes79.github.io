World Science Day

Celebrated every 10 November, World Science Day for Peace and Development highlights the significant role of science in society and the need to engage the wider public in debates on emerging scientific issues. It also underlines the importance and relevance of science in our daily lives.

By linking science more closely with society, World Science Day for Peace and Development aims to ensure that citizens are kept informed of developments in science. It also underscores the role scientists play in broadening our understanding of the remarkable, fragile planet we call home and in making our societies more sustainable.

The Purpose of this Day is to:

The day represents the fact that science should not only be a collection of information, but an instrument used to solve world problems. It shares common scientific practices and invests in science education. World Science Day integrates scientific knowledge into community needs so as to develop an informative public that can react to scientific issues critically.

Individuals and institutions are also encouraged to organize debates, educational activities, and cultural events that exalt the contribution of science to peace and development. The celebration this year will coincide with the International Decade of Sciences for Sustainable Development (2024-2033) to highlight science as the means through which sustainability objectives would be accomplished.
